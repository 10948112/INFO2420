Web Site Critiques

Evaluation Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Trader Joe’s – www.traderjoes.com

First Impression: My first thought was that it looks kind of fun and colorful. It feels different from a normal grocery store site.

Information Flow: The main links are across the top (products, recipes, etc.). It was easy to see where things are.

Structure: The layout is simple and makes sense. I didn’t get lost or confused.

Graphics/Layout: The pictures are bright and make the page look more interesting. They don’t get in the way.

Load Time: It loaded pretty quick on my computer, just a second or two.

Text Readability: The text was fine to read. The background is plain so nothing clashed.

Navigation: Yes, there’s a bar at the top for navigation. It worked fine for me.

Color Scheme: The site mainly uses red and white. The same look shows up on all the pages I clicked.

Responsive Design: When I made the window smaller, it adjusted so I could still read everything. Looked okay on my phone too.

Unusual/Well Done: The “Fearless Flyer” part stood out. It’s like a mini magazine and kind of fun to scroll through.
